Purpose

To understand the mechanisms of pretraining optimization and scope out what the frontier methods are. This includes AdamW, Muon, and MoE. This information should directly inform what type of training we conduct for the final eduLLM.

Out of scope

Anything outside of pretraining optimization.

DOK4 — SPOVs

SPOV 1: The pretraining method should be heavily adapted to the type of learning the model needs to do. Since it will be absorbing knowledge from textbooks / curriculum-shaped data, we should not default to a generic web-crawl AdamW recipe without asking what geometry that data creates.

SPOV 2: The loss function and frontier training method should heavily depend on the fact that we are training an eduLLM. Priority is a model that teaches students well under budget, not generic perplexity. Learning-science techniques (skill order, worked examples, QA form) belong in the pretraining conversation, not only in post-training or UX.

Sources

Source 1 — Why Momentum Really Works (Distill)

distill.pub/2017/momentum · math notes
Deep-read

DOK1 — Facts

  • Pathological curvature: axes mismatched in curvature. Measured by $\kappa=\lambda_{\max}/\lambda_{\min}$.
  • On a convex quadratic, GD in the eigenbasis updates each mode independently: $x_i^{k+1}=(1-\alpha\lambda_i)x_i^k$.
  • Momentum uses a velocity state. Each mode becomes a 2D linear system. Optimal rate scales like $(\sqrt{\kappa}-1)/(\sqrt{\kappa}+1)$.
  • First-order methods are linear combinations of past gradients. On Distill’s hard path, that implies a speed-of-light limit on information transfer.
  • As $n\to\infty$ on that path objective, $\kappa(f^n)$ approaches the design $\kappa$, and momentum’s upper bound meets the lower bound.
  • Escape hatch: reformulate to change $\kappa$ / geometry. Don’t only remix gradient coefficients.

DOK2 — Summary. Eigenbasis thinking makes GD/momentum clearer: modes decouple, $\kappa$ explains the pain, and momentum’s $\sqrt{\kappa}$ speedup is real but basically tight for linear first-order methods on that worst-case class. For eduLLM pretraining: cursed geometry will not be fixed by a cleverer first-order trick alone.
